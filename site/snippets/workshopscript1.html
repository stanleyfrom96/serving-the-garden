Introduction ⁺₊⋆

Hi everyone, First of all, thank you for giving us the opportunity to divide our workshop into two parts. It means a lot to us.

We have thought carefully about how to approach the topic of the “server” in a way that goes beyond an extractivist perspective. Rather than seeing the server merely as a tool “for us,” we want to consider it as a shared space that requires nurturing.

When our group first tried to build a server, we found ourselves asking: What exactly is a server*? At that time, Asya gave us a simple yet clear answer: “The server is someone else’s computer.” In today’s meeting, we want to challenge the idea of accessing the server space by using the metaphor of a piece of land.

In this workshop “someone else’s computer” will be transformed into a collective garden, encouraging us to think aloud about what is shared, where it is, and how we can care for it.

By using this metaphor, we aim to avoid the constraints of programming language and make servers a more tangible space. Seeing the server as a garden also helps us raise questions about accessibility and needs.

The Garden of Many Roots ❀ ⁺₊⋆

Scene Narrator: Description of the surrondings
arrator: A wise and thoughtful voice guiding the story.
Pip: A clever but lazy squirrel.
Fox: A patient and insightful character, dedicated to the garden.
Mole: A practical and hardworking digger, focused on the garden's foundation.
Sparrow: A cheerful and optimistic bird with a strong sense of community.
Owl: A thoughtful and wise observer who asks probing questions.

Narrator: (calm and reflective) Once upon a time, in a forest not so far from here, there was a squirrel named Pip. Quick, clever, and just a bit lazy, Pip loved taking shortcuts. Why work hard when there is an easier way?
Scene Narrator: Pip sits under a tree with a shiny red apple, its core tossed aside. The distant hum of silver towers echoes in the background.
Narrator: (amused) Pip’s favorite place was the big market at the edge of the forest — a land of silver towers humming softly under the sun, filled with rows of fruit, ripe and ready.
>Pip:(muttering, inspecting the empty apple core) Strange... no seeds.
Scene Narrator: He tosses the core away and notices a flash of red fur in the distance.
Narrator: Curious as ever, Pip scurried toward the clearing, where a fox was digging in the dry soil.
Pip:(inquisitive) What are you doing?
Fox: (without looking up) Planting a seed.
Pip:(chuckling) Why bother? The market has all the fruit you’d ever need—no digging required!
Scene Narrator: The fox pauses, brushing dust from his nose, and points to the silver towers on the horizon.
Fox: The market’s fruit has no seeds, Pip. Without seeds, there’s no future. And without roots, nothing remembers.
Pip:(skeptical) What’s there to remember? It’s just fruit.
Fox: (smiling knowingly) Come back tomorrow. I’ll show you.

Scene Narrator: The clearing the next day. The fox places a seed into the dry soil, carefully covering it with dirt.
Pip:(crossing his arms) This will never work. The market is faster and easier.
Fox: (gently) The soil needs time to remember.
Pip:(frowning) Remember what?
Fox: That someone cared enough to plant a seed.
Narrator: Days pass. Pip grows frustrated, but one morning, he gasps at the sight of a tiny green sprout breaking through the soil.
Pip:(excited) It’s alive!
Fox: (nodding) The soil remembers.

Scene Narrator: More animals gather in the garden. The mole digs tunnels beneath the soil, the sparrow plants seeds, and the owl observes from above.
Mole: (wiping dirt from her claws) Roots need space to stretch—like making paths for wires underground.
Sparrow: (chirping brightly) More seeds mean stronger connections.
Narrator: As the garden grows, questions emerge.
Owl: Who decides what grows?
Mole: Who digs the deepest burrows?
Fox: And how do we make sure no one takes more than their share?
Scene Narrator: Voices overlap, arguments flare. The garden seems at risk of falling apart until the animals begin to listen.
Narrator: Under the shade of the tallest plants, the animals gathered in circles, sharing hopes and struggles.
Scene Narrator: The fox stands, holding a fruit.
Fox: This garden belongs to everyone who has cared for it—and everyone who will care for it. But care is not equal. Some bear a heavier weight. What matters is not avoiding the burden but learning to share it.
Scene Narrator: Silence falls. Faces thoughtful, the animals nod.
Owl: (to Pip) What does it mean to share care? Can it ever be fair?
Scene Narrator: Pip stares at the sprout, uncertain.

Scene Narrator: The garden in full bloom. Mole shows Pip the intricate web of roots beneath the soil.
Mole: (reverently) They remember every storm, every pawprint, every claw. They speak—not in words, but in how they grow together.
Pip: (whispering) What do they say?
Mole: They say, “Thank you.” But they also ask, “What will you do next?”
Scene Narrator: The camera lingers on Pip, staring at the roots as the garden thrives above.
Narrator: And so the question remains—not just for Pip, but for us all.
Narrator and everyone together: (in unison, softly) What will you do next? And who will you do it with?
(Fade to black.)
